The Emotional Burden of Being Hacked

After hackers leaked private photos and data, many victims described the experience as a profound invasion of privacy and emotional trauma. Hacking can bring stress, anxiety, and a sense of powerlessness that stays long after the breach occurs.

Experts say the delay in notifying victims about breaches often worsens psychological distress, with many people feeling uncertain, violated, and overwhelmed by what was exposed.

Victims report difficulty sleeping, trust issues with institutions, and a lingering sense of vulnerability even after accounts are recovered.
